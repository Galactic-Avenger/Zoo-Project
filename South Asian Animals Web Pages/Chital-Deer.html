Chital Deer

The spotted deer or chital deer is a species of deer found in the Indian subcontinent.

The chital deer is a medium-sized deer with a reddish-brown coat and white spots. It is a herbivore and feeds on grass, leaves, and fruits. The chital deer is a social animal and lives in herds. It is a very fast runner and can run at a speed of up to 60 km/h. The chital deer is a very good swimmer and can swim long distances. It is a very good jumper and can jump over obstacles up to 2 meters high. It is a very good climber and can climb trees with ease. The chital deer is a very good prey animal and is hunted by predators like tigers, leopards, and wild dogs.
