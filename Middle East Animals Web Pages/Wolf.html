Arabian Wolf

The Arabian Wolf (Canis lupus arabs) is a small and highly adaptable subspecies of the gray wolf found in the Middle East. Unlike larger wolves, it is known for hunting alone or in pairs rather than in packs.

It feeds on rodents, hares, and sometimes livestock, making it a common target for farmers. Due to habitat destruction and hunting, its population has declined significantly.

Conservation efforts are focused on protecting Arabian Wolves through wildlife reserves and educational programs to reduce human-wildlife conflicts.
