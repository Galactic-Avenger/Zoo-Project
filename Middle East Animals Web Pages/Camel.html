Dromedary Camel

The Dromedary Camel, also known as the Arabian Camel, is a one-humped species adapted to **desert life**. It is famous for its ability to **survive without water for long periods**, thanks to its **efficient water storage system**.

Unlike popular belief, the camel's hump does not store water but **fat reserves**, which can be converted into energy and hydration. Their long legs and padded feet allow them to walk effortlessly on **hot desert sand**, and their thick eyelashes and closable nostrils protect them from sandstorms.

Camels have been an **essential part of Arabian culture for centuries**, used for **transportation, milk production, and trade**. Today, they remain **highly valued in desert regions** and are often seen in camel races and traditional festivities.
